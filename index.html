A Tribute to Zhou Youguang

The Father of Pinyin

Zhou in 2012 (image credit: Wikipedia)

   

Zhou Youguang (1906-2017) was a Chinese linguist, writer, and sometime dissident, among other things. The achievement he is most famous for is the invention of Pinyin, a transliteration/romanization system for the Chinese language. When you read words like "Beijing" or "feng shui", you're reading Pinyin.

A timeline of Zhou Youguang's life:
  • 1906Born in Changzhou, Jiangsu province.
  • 1927Graduates from Guanghua University.
  • 1933Marries his wife, Zhang Yunhe, and moves to Japan for his studies.
  • 1937Due to the outbreak of the Second Sino-Japanese War, moves back to China, this time to Chongqing. His daughter dies.
  • 1945Japan is defeated. Zhou works abroard with Sin Hua Bank in New York and London.
  • 1955The Chinese Communist Party places him at the head of a language-reform comittee. During this time, he works on developing Pinyin.
  • 1958Pinyin starts being promoted as the official romanization for Chinese.
  • 196?Branded a counterrevolutionary and sent to the countryside for "reeducation" during the Cultural Revolution.
  • 1980Starts working on translating the Encyclopædia Britannica into Chinese.
  • 2000Starts work on the first of ten books on a variety of subjects.
  • 2011Controversially criticizes former paramount leader Deng Xiaoping in an interview with NPR.
  • 2017Dies at his home in Beijing. He was 111 years and 1 day old, and had outlived both his wife and his son.
